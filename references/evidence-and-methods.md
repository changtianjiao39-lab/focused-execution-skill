# Evidence and Method Boundaries

Use these sources to explain why an intervention was selected. Prefer modest claims and distinguish behavioral evidence, association, and neural correlates from causal treatment evidence.

## Mechanisms

- A small fMRI study linked task aversiveness and outcome value to different neural pathways. It supports addressing both the unpleasantness of acting now and the value of the later outcome, but it does not prove a single “procrastination center” or a brain hack. Source: https://academic.oup.com/cercor/article-abstract/31/8/3846/6220169
- A 2025 study connected procrastination with attentional control, mind-wandering, and emotion dysregulation. Treat these as related mechanisms, not proof of a diagnosis. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40296374/

## Task choice and switching

- Six years of hospital data found that heavier workload can increase preference for easier tasks and that this can hurt performance. This supports an external priority rule instead of choosing by ease. Source: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2019.3419
- Experiments found that switching away from unfinished work can leave attention residue and impair the next task. Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0749597809000399
- Experiments found that a brief ready-to-resume plan can mitigate residue when interruption is unavoidable. Source: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/orsc.2017.1184
- A field experiment associated fewer notification-caused interruptions with better work performance, while blanket blocking can also create anxiety for some people. Prefer selective blocking and observe the result. Source: https://academic.oup.com/joh/article/65/1/e12408/7479297

## Planning and action

- Process simulation improved planning and performance more reliably than imagining only a successful outcome in an examination experiment. Source: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0146167299025002010
- Self-imposed deadlines can reduce procrastination, but externally structured deadlines performed better in the original experiments. Prefer early evidence checkpoints over a single final deadline. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12009041/
- If-then plans can help initiation and goal shielding in some studies. They are not sufficient by themselves: another experiment found no reduction in overall academic procrastination from SMART goals or implementation intentions alone. Sources: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18272806/ and https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5608091/
- Public recognition of identity-related intentions can sometimes reduce subsequent action. Prefer artifact-based accountability over statements such as “I am becoming disciplined.” Source: https://www.psychologicalscience.org/journals/psychological-science/j.1467-9280.2009.02336.x/

## Perfectionism and iteration

- A seven-day diary study found that perfectionistic concerns were associated with the perceived gap between actual and ideal performance, which in turn related to procrastination. Do not equate healthy striving with harmful perfectionism. Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0191886917301484

## Intervention strength

- Guided, multi-session interventions have stronger behavior-change evidence than a single productivity tip. A randomized trial with 403 participants found that a guided online intervention reduced procrastination, with effects maintained at follow-up. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41080972/
- A single-session digital intervention did not produce a differential reduction in self-reported procrastination in one randomized trial. Do not promise that one focus card will solve a persistent pattern. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38623085/
- A brief study combining affect labeling, subtask generation, and reward selection improved motivational outcomes, but did not establish durable real-world completion for every person. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41102857/

## Sleep and diagnostic boundary

- Sleep restriction can impair vigilance, processing speed, and some executive functions. Do not normalize all-night work as a productivity method. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30425658/
- Adult ADHD assessment considers a persistent developmental pattern, impairment in multiple settings, and alternative explanations. Do not infer ADHD from distraction or procrastination alone. Source: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/adhd-what-you-need-to-know

## Evidence-aware coaching rule

Use research to choose a small behavioral experiment, then judge usefulness from observed behavior. Track start delay, task switches, visible artifacts, missed checkpoints, and sleep cost—not only how motivated someone felt.
